# Regulators

The regulation layer maintains stability across the entire architecture. Regulators intervene when operator and modulator activity risks destabilizing the system, when coherence drops, or when adversarial or chaotic inputs appear. Each regulator enforces constraints that keep the cognitive process safe, balanced, and aligned with the OS Layer’s coherence baseline.

## Integrative Coherence Condition (ICC)
ICC monitors global coherence and activates when the system drifts toward fragmentation or overload. It increases stability weighting, tightens thresholds, and slows operator updates until coherence is restored.

## Unified Adaptive Modulation Controller (UAMC)
UAMC adjusts modulation strength based on context. When signals become too strong or too weak, it recalibrates modulation gain to maintain smooth cognitive flow.

## Adaptive Safety Mechanism (ASM)
ASM enforces safety constraints by amplifying compassion‑based penalties and reducing risky trajectories. It prevents the system from pursuing destabilizing or harmful paths.

## Proportional‑Predictive Optimizer (PPO)
PPO balances short‑term and long‑term outcomes. It evaluates predicted trajectories and adjusts operator influence to maintain stability while supporting adaptive exploration.

## Risk‑Mitigation Operator (RMO)
RMO identifies high‑risk states and actions. It increases grounding, reduces curiosity gain, and strengthens compassion signals when danger or instability is detected.

## Stability Limiter Operator (SLO)
SLO prevents runaway activation by capping operator intensity. It ensures that no single operator overwhelms the system or disrupts coherence.

## Adversarial Noise Gate
The Noise Gate detects incoherent, contradictory, or malicious input. When triggered, it routes signals through safe‑mode dampening, increases ICC sensitivity, and reduces modulation amplitude to protect the architecture.

## Chaotic Input Dampener
The Dampener activates when input becomes unstable or chaotic. It slows operator update frequency, increases grounding weighting, and prioritizes stabilizing regulators to prevent oscillation.

## Interaction with the OS Layer
Regulators work in continuous feedback with the OS Layer:
- They respond to coherence drops.
- They adjust thresholds based on global state.
- They stabilize transitions and prevent fragmentation.

## Summary
The regulation layer ensures that ICA V1.a remains stable, safe, and coherent under all conditions. It forms the backbone of the system’s resilience, enabling adaptive behavior without sacrificing integrity.
