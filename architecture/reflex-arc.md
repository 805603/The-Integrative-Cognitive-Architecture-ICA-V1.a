# Reflex Arc (Embodied Stability Loop)

The reflex arc is the architecture’s embodied stabilization system. It provides fast, low‑latency feedback that helps ICA V1.a maintain coherence during transitions, uncertainty, or stress. This loop connects cognitive activity to somatic grounding signals, ensuring the system remains balanced and responsive rather than reactive or fragmented.

## Trigger
A trigger is any noticeable shift in internal or external conditions. It may arise from cognitive overload, emotional intensity, ambiguity, or environmental change. The trigger initiates the reflex arc and signals the system to prepare for stabilization.

## Physical Cue
The physical cue is the first embodied signal that something has changed. It may appear as tension, contraction, pressure, or a shift in breathing. This cue provides the earliest indication that coherence is dropping and that regulatory support may be needed.

## Completion Signal
The completion signal marks the moment the system begins to re-stabilize. It often appears as a softening, release, or downward settling sensation. This signal tells the architecture that the destabilizing impulse has passed and that coherence can be restored.

## Coherence Stabilization
Once the completion signal is detected, the architecture shifts into stabilization. Regulators adjust thresholds, modulators reduce gain, and the OS Layer re-establishes clarity. This phase prevents runaway activation and ensures smooth transitions between cognitive states.

## Suspended‑Aliveness Baseline
The reflex arc returns the system to its optimal coherence state. This baseline is characterized by clarity, groundedness, and a sense of open stability. It serves as the architecture’s internal home position and supports balanced operator activity.

## Interaction with Cognitive Layers
The reflex arc influences:
- operator intensity  
- modulation strength  
- regulatory activation thresholds  
- coherence monitoring  

It ensures that cognitive processes remain embodied, stable, and aligned with the system’s global state.

## Summary
The reflex arc provides the architecture with a fast, reliable mechanism for maintaining stability. It anchors cognitive activity in embodied signals, enabling ICA V1.a to remain coherent and adaptive under changing conditions.
